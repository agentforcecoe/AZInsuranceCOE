Who We Are

Protecting Families Since 1998

AZ Insurance was founded in 1998 in Singapore with a singular mission: to make world-class insurance protection accessible to every family in Southeast Asia. What began as a boutique life insurance firm with a team of 40 has grown into a regional powerhouse with over 3,200 employees, 500,000 active policyholders, and dual regulatory licences in Singapore and the Philippines.

Our Singapore operations are fully regulated and supervised by the Monetary Authority of Singapore (MAS), while our Philippine entity — AZ Insurance Corporation — holds a Certificate of Authority from the Insurance Commission (IC) and is a registered PDIC member institution. We maintain an AA credit rating from Fitch Ratings, reflecting our long-term financial strength.

At AZ Insurance, we believe that insurance is ultimately a promise — a commitment to be there when life takes an unexpected turn. Every product we build, every policy we issue, and every claim we settle is guided by that core belief. We invest heavily in our people, our technology, and our customer experience so that promise is never just ink on paper.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about AZ Insurance products, claims, and policies.

What is a participating insurance policy?+
A participating policy means your premiums are pooled into a participating fund managed by AZ Insurance. In addition to guaranteed benefits, you are eligible to receive non-guaranteed bonuses — reversionary bonuses declared annually and a terminal bonus paid at claim, surrender, or maturity. These bonuses reflect the performance of the participating fund and are not contractually guaranteed.
When does my policy start building cash value?+
For AZ Legacy Life Assurance, the policy begins accumulating guaranteed cash value after the completion of the second policy year. The guaranteed cash value ledger is generated at policy inception and attached to your policy contract so you can see projected values at each policy anniversary.
What happens if I miss a premium payment?+
AZ Insurance provides a 31-day grace period from the premium due date at no additional cost. If the premium remains unpaid after the grace period, the Automatic Premium Loan (APL) feature activates, advancing the overdue amount as a loan against your accumulated cash value (provided sufficient cash value exists). Interest accrues daily at prevailing market rates. If the total outstanding loan and interest exceed the total cash value, the policy will lapse.
Can I change my beneficiaries?+
Yes. Beneficiary changes can be made at any time during the policy term by submitting a Beneficiary Change Form (Form BC-04) to any AZ Insurance branch or via the MyAZ portal. Note that for irrevocable beneficiary designations, the consent of all irrevocable beneficiaries is required before a change can be processed.
What is the TPD definition under AZ Legacy Life?+
Total and Permanent Disability (TPD) is defined as the total and irrecoverable loss of sight in both eyes, or the loss of use of two limbs, or the inability to perform all Activities of Daily Living (ADLs) for a continuous period of at least six months, as certified by an independent specialist. The TPD benefit advances 100% of the Death Benefit and is available up to the policy anniversary nearest the Life Assured's 65th birthday.
Are AZ Insurance products available to non-citizens / foreigners?+
Yes. In Singapore, AZ Insurance products are available to Singapore Citizens, Permanent Residents, and valid Employment Pass / S Pass / Dependent Pass holders. In the Philippines, most products are available to foreign nationals with valid residency status. Additional underwriting requirements may apply. Please speak to an advisor for your specific situation.
How do surrender charges work?+
If you surrender your AZ Legacy Life Assurance policy within the first 10 policy years, a reduced cash value matrix applies. The precise surrender value at each policy year is specified in the Surrender Value Ledger attached to your policy contract at inception. After the 10th policy year, full surrender value (inclusive of accumulated reversionary bonuses) applies. Early surrender is generally inadvisable as the policy is structured for long-term value accumulation.
How long does a claim take to process?+
AZ Insurance is committed to settling 98% of straightforward claims within 5 business days of receiving a complete set of documents. Complex claims requiring specialist medical review or investigation may take longer. Your dedicated claims manager will keep you updated at every stage and provide an expected timeline within 2 business days of notification.
