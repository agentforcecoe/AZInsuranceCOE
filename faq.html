📋 Policy Basics
What is a participating insurance policy? +
A participating policy means your premiums are pooled into a participating fund managed by AZ Insurance. In addition to guaranteed benefits, you are eligible to receive non-guaranteed bonuses — reversionary bonuses declared annually and a terminal bonus paid at claim, surrender, or maturity. These bonuses reflect the performance of the participating fund and are not contractually guaranteed.
What is the difference between a participating and non-participating policy? +
A participating policy shares in the profits of the AZ Participating Fund through non-guaranteed bonuses — your returns can grow beyond the guaranteed sum assured. A non-participating policy provides fixed, guaranteed benefits only, with no bonus upside. Products like AZ Shield Term Essential and AZ Peso Harvest 10-5 are non-participating, offering certainty and simplicity. Products like AZ Legacy Life Assurance and AZ Golden Years Retirement Income are participating, offering growth potential alongside guarantees.
Can I change my beneficiaries after the policy is issued? +
Yes. Beneficiary changes can be made at any time during the policy term by submitting a Beneficiary Change Form (Form BC-04) to any AZ Insurance branch or via the MyAZ portal. Note that for irrevocable beneficiary designations, the consent of all irrevocable beneficiaries is required before a change can be processed.
Are AZ Insurance products available to non-citizens or foreigners? +
Yes. In Singapore, AZ Insurance products are available to Singapore Citizens, Permanent Residents, and valid Employment Pass / S Pass / Dependent Pass holders. In the Philippines, most products are available to foreign nationals with valid residency status. Additional underwriting requirements may apply. Please speak to an advisor for your specific situation.
What is the minimum age to take out a policy? +
Policy owner minimum age is 18 years for most AZ Insurance products. The life assured's minimum age varies by product — as young as 15 days for AZ Legacy Life Assurance and AZ Shield Term Essential (16 years). For Philippines products like AZ Fit and Well Protect, children can be insured from age 1 year, and AZ Future Scholars Builder covers children from 30 days up to 12 years. Please refer to each product's eligibility table for specifics.
How do I access my policy documents? +
Your policy contract, benefit illustration, and surrender value ledger are issued in physical and digital form at policy inception. All documents are accessible 24/7 through the MyAZ mobile app and client portal at my.azinsurance.com. For Singapore policies, you will also receive notifications via your registered email and Singpass Inbox for policy updates and renewal notices.
💳 Premiums & Payments
What happens if I miss a premium payment? +
AZ Insurance provides a 31-day grace period from the premium due date at no additional cost. If the premium remains unpaid after the grace period, the Automatic Premium Loan (APL) feature activates for eligible policies, advancing the overdue amount as a loan against your accumulated cash value (provided sufficient cash value exists). Interest accrues daily at prevailing market rates. If the total outstanding loan and interest exceed the total cash value, the policy will lapse. For pure protection policies like AZ Shield Term Essential with no cash value, the policy lapses directly after the grace period.
What payment channels are accepted? +
Singapore products accept Interbank GIRO, PayNow, major credit cards, and AXS online payments. Philippines products accept GCash, Maya, ShopeePay, over-the-counter bank payments, credit cards, and auto-debit arrangements from BDO, BPI, UnionBank, and Metrobank. Bancassurance products distributed through BPI or Metrobank are settled directly via linked bank accounts or Manager's Checks.
Can I change my premium payment frequency? +
Yes. Most AZ Insurance products support monthly, quarterly, semi-annual, and annual payment modes. You can switch your payment frequency once per policy year by submitting a Premium Mode Change Request via the MyAZ portal or at any AZ Insurance branch. The change takes effect from the next premium due date. Note that monthly premiums may carry a small loading charge compared to annual payments.
How do surrender charges work? +
Surrender charges apply differently depending on the product. For AZ Legacy Life Assurance, a reduced cash value matrix applies within the first 10 policy years — the precise values are in the Surrender Value Ledger attached to your policy. For VUL products like AZ Maxilink Prime, surrender charges apply in the first 5 years (5% in Year 1, reducing to 0% from Year 6). AZ Horizon Wealth Capital Plus charges surrender fees only for the first 3 years. Pure term policies like AZ Shield Term Essential have zero surrender value at all times. For bancassurance products like AZ BPI Secure Wealth, early exit penalties apply from 5% in Year 1 down to 1% in Year 5.
Can I reinstate a lapsed policy? +
Yes. For most AZ Insurance life and health policies, reinstatement is allowed within 2 years from the lapse date. You will need to settle all outstanding past-due premiums with accumulated interest and submit a new evidence of insurability health declaration. The reinstatement is subject to AZ Insurance's underwriting approval. Reinstatement is not available for general insurance (motor, travel, home) annual policies — these require a new application.
⚡ Claims
How long does a claim take to process? +
AZ Insurance is committed to settling 98% of straightforward claims within 5 business days of receiving a complete set of documents. Complex claims requiring specialist medical review or investigation may take longer. Your dedicated claims manager will keep you updated at every stage and provide an expected timeline within 2 business days of notification.
How do I file a claim? +
You can file a claim through three channels: (1) Call our 24/7 claims hotline — +65 6222 0000 for Singapore or +63 2 8888 0000 for Philippines; (2) Submit online via the MyAZ portal at my.azinsurance.com; or (3) Visit any AZ Insurance branch in person. You must notify us within 30 days of the claimable event. Your assigned claims manager will then send you a personalised document checklist within 1 business day.
What documents are needed for a life insurance claim? +
For a death claim, you will need: the completed AZ Claim Form (Form LC-01), the original policy document or policy number, a certified death certificate, NRIC or passport of the claimant and beneficiary, and the beneficiary's bank account details for disbursement. For a TPD claim, you additionally need medical reports and specialist certification confirming the disability meets the policy's definition of Total and Permanent Disability.
Can a claim be rejected? What are common reasons? +
Yes, claims can be declined in specific circumstances. Common reasons include: the claimable event falls within an exclusion (e.g. self-inflicted injury, pre-existing conditions not disclosed at application, incidents under the influence of alcohol or narcotics, or professional motorsports); the policy has lapsed due to non-payment; the waiting period has not been satisfied (e.g. the 90-day waiting period for AZ Cancer Shield Care); or required documentation is incomplete. AZ Insurance will always communicate the specific reason for any decline in writing.
How is the claim payout disbursed? +
All approved claim amounts are disbursed via direct bank transfer to the designated beneficiary's or claimant's registered bank account within 5 business days of claim approval. For Singapore policies, payment is made in SGD via FAST or GIRO. For Philippines policies, payment is in PHP via the registered local bank account. For the AZ Pinnacle Universal Sovereign HNW policy, death benefit payouts are settled in USD to the designated estate trustee account.
🛡️ Life Insurance
When does my policy start building cash value? +
For AZ Legacy Life Assurance, the policy begins accumulating guaranteed cash value after the completion of the second policy year. The guaranteed cash value ledger is generated at policy inception and attached to your policy contract so you can see projected values at each policy anniversary. For AZ Golden Years Retirement Income, cash value accumulates throughout the savings phase and transitions into guaranteed monthly income payouts from your chosen retirement age.
What is the TPD definition under AZ Legacy Life Assurance? +
Total and Permanent Disability (TPD) is defined as the total and irrecoverable loss of sight in both eyes, or the loss of use of two limbs, or the inability to perform all Activities of Daily Living (ADLs) for a continuous period of at least six months, as certified by an independent specialist. The TPD benefit advances 100% of the Death Benefit and is available up to the policy anniversary nearest the Life Assured's 65th birthday.
What are reversionary bonuses and terminal bonuses? +
A reversionary bonus is a non-guaranteed annual addition to your policy's face value. Once declared by AZ Insurance, it becomes permanent — it cannot be taken away or reduced. Over time, reversionary bonuses compound significantly. A terminal bonus is a separate non-guaranteed one-time payment made only when a claim is triggered — at death, surrender, or policy maturity. It is calculated as a percentage of the total accumulated reversionary bonuses and is declared by the company actuary at the time of the event.
What is the difference between AZ Legacy Life Assurance and AZ Shield Term Essential? +
AZ Legacy Life Assurance is a whole life participating policy — it provides lifetime coverage to age 99, builds guaranteed cash value from Year 2, and earns non-guaranteed reversionary and terminal bonuses. Premiums are higher but the policy doubles as a savings and estate planning tool. AZ Shield Term Essential is a pure term policy — it provides high coverage (from SGD 500,000) for a defined term at much lower premiums, with zero cash value. It is best suited for income replacement, mortgage protection, or covering specific financial liabilities over a fixed horizon.
Can I take a loan against my life insurance policy? +
Yes. For policies with accumulated cash value — such as AZ Legacy Life Assurance and AZ Golden Years Retirement Income — you can apply for a policy loan of up to 90% of the current guaranteed cash value. Interest accrues daily at prevailing rates on the outstanding loan balance. The Automatic Premium Loan (APL) feature also uses this mechanism to pay unpaid premiums automatically. For AZ GrowSure Endowment, the policy loan facility allows borrowing up to 80% of cash surrender value.
❤️ Health & Critical Illness
What is the waiting period for critical illness and cancer policies? +
Both AZ Fit and Well Protect and AZ Cancer Shield Care enforce a 90-day waiting period from the policy effective date. No benefits are payable for any critical illness or cancer diagnosed within this 90-day window. AZ Cancer Shield Care additionally imposes a 14-day survival clause — the policyholder must survive at least 14 days after diagnosis before a claim can be liquidated. These waiting periods are standard across the industry and are in place to prevent adverse selection.
Does AZ Fit and Well Protect cover pre-existing conditions? +
Pre-existing conditions known at the time of application are subject to underwriting review. Depending on the nature and severity of the condition, AZ Insurance may offer coverage with an exclusion rider, charge a higher premium, or decline coverage. Conditions that first arise after the policy's effective date and after the 90-day waiting period are fully covered, provided all disclosures were made honestly at application. Non-disclosure of material health information may result in claim rejection or policy cancellation.
What does AZ Corporate Care Pro cover for employees? +
AZ Corporate Care Pro provides three tiers of group health coverage: (1) Inpatient Hospitalisation — up to SGD 50,000 (standard tier) or SGD 250,000 (executive tier) in Singapore restructured hospitals; (2) GP Outpatient Care — full coverage at 500+ panel clinics with a SGD 5 employee co-payment per visit; and (3) Specialist Outpatient — up to SGD 5,000 per year with a GP referral. The policy is issued to the employer and covers all eligible full-time employees aged 16 to 65, with a minimum group size of 5 staff.
Is the Healthy Living Cash Back guaranteed under AZ Fit and Well Protect? +
Yes. If the policyholder reaches the maturity age (either 75 or 100, depending on the plan chosen at inception) without having made any critical illness claim, AZ Insurance will pay a guaranteed cash payout equal to 100% of the total premiums paid across the lifetime of the policy. This is a contractual guarantee, not a non-guaranteed bonus. It provides a zero-loss floor for health-conscious policyholders who maintain their well-being throughout the policy term.
📈 Investment & VUL
What is a Variable Universal Life (VUL) policy? +
A Variable Universal Life (VUL) policy combines life insurance protection with an investment component. Premiums, after deducting insurance charges and fees, are invested into chosen sub-funds (e.g. equity, balanced, or fixed income). The total cash value of your policy grows or falls based on the net asset value of the underlying funds — unlike traditional policies, returns are not guaranteed. AZ offers two VUL products: AZ Maxilink Prime (regular premium, Philippines) and AZ Horizon Wealth Capital Plus (single premium, Singapore).
Can I switch funds within my VUL policy? +
Yes. For AZ Maxilink Prime, fund switching is free of charge for up to 10 times per calendar year. For AZ Horizon Wealth Capital Plus (Singapore), fund switching is completely unlimited and always free via the My AZ Digital Portal. Fund switches are executed at the next available Net Asset Value Per Unit (NAVPU) price following the submission of the request. You can shift between equity, balanced, fixed income, and other available sub-funds to match your changing risk appetite.
What is a Premium Holiday and when can I use it? +
Available exclusively on AZ Maxilink Prime, a Premium Holiday allows you to formally pause premium payments for up to 24 consecutive months, provided you have completed at least 3 full consecutive policy years. During the holiday, AZ Insurance continues to deduct the Cost of Insurance and Policy Maintenance Charges via monthly unit cancellation from your fund value. If the total account value drops to zero during the holiday, a 31-day grace period is triggered — failure to resume payments will result in policy lapse.
What is the difference between AZ Prosperity Fund Accumulator and AZ Maxilink Prime? +
AZ Prosperity Fund Accumulator is a pure retail mutual fund with no life insurance component — 100% of your capital goes into institutional investment instruments (Equity, Balanced, or Money Market). It is regulated by the SEC Philippines and is ideal for pure wealth accumulation. AZ Maxilink Prime is a VUL insurance policy — it combines life insurance protection (minimum PHP 200,000 death benefit) with investment in the same fund types. VUL involves insurance charges deducted from premiums. If pure investment growth without insurance is your goal, the Prosperity Fund Accumulator is more cost-efficient; if you need life coverage alongside wealth building, Maxilink Prime is more appropriate.
What fees apply to the AZ Institutional Income Bond Master fund? +
The AZ Institutional Income Bond Master charges a 0.75% front-end sales charge on gross investment, a 0.50% exit redemption charge if liquidated within 90 days of purchase (zero after 90 days), and a Total Expense Ratio (TER) of 0.85% per annum calculated and adjusted daily within the fund's declared Net Asset Value Per Unit. There is no account setup fee. The minimum initial investment is SGD 25,000 with a minimum top-up of SGD 5,000 per transaction.
🚗 Motor, Travel & Home
How does the No Claims Discount (NCD) work for motor insurance? +
AZ Auto Drive Comprehensive follows the standard Singapore NCD scale: 1 claim-free year earns 10% discount, 2 years gives 20%, 3 years gives 30%, 4 years gives 40%, and 5 or more claim-free years earns the maximum 50% discount. If you file a claim during a policy year, your NCD is reduced at renewal according to the prevailing NCD scale. Your NCD is portable — it applies to the driver, not the vehicle, and transfers when you switch insurers.
Does AZ Global Assure Travel cover Schengen visa requirements? +
Yes. AZ Global Assure Travel (Zone C plan) meets the mandatory EUR 30,000 minimum medical insurance requirement for Schengen visa applications. The policy provides emergency medical coverage of up to PHP 2,500,000 for the trip, which exceeds the Schengen threshold. AZ Insurance provides the required insurance certificate letter for visa submission. The policy must cover the entire duration of the intended Schengen stay, including travel days.
What is excluded under AZ Home Fortress Secure? +
AZ Home Fortress Secure does not cover: normal structural wear and tear, gradual moisture or water seepage damage, war risks, nuclear contamination, assets stolen without evidence of forced entry into the property, or wooden and light-material structures (only concrete residential properties are eligible). A statutory catastrophe deductible of 2% of the item sum insured applies to earthquake and typhoon claims, as mandated by regional regulatory boards. Commercial structures and warehouses are also excluded.
What is the excess for AZ Auto Drive Comprehensive motor claims? +
The standard excess for own damage repairs under AZ Auto Drive Comprehensive is SGD 600 per claim — you pay the first SGD 600 and AZ Insurance covers the rest. An additional young/inexperienced driver excess of SGD 500 applies if the driver at the time of the accident is under 25 years old or holds a licence with less than 2 years of driving experience. This excess is on top of the standard SGD 600, bringing the total excess to SGD 1,100 for young drivers.
